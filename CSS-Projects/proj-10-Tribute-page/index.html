Ada Lovelace

The first programmer in history

Time line of Ada Lovelace's life:

  • 1815 (December 10) - Ada Lovelace is born in London, England, as Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace, to Anne Isabella Milbanke and Lord Byron.
  • Early Education - Ada receives a strong education, particularly in mathematics and science, due to the efforts of her mother.
  • 1831 - Ada's family meets Charles Babbage, a prominent mathematician and inventor, who is working on the Difference Engine, an early calculating machine.
  • 1835 - Ada marries William King, who later becomes the Earl of Lovelace, and Ada becomes the Countess of Lovelace.
  • 1838 - Ada becomes a mother.
  • 1839 - Ada starts to collaborate with Babbage on the Analytical Engine, a more advanced calculating machine.
  • 1843 - Ada writes a comprehensive note on Babbage's Analytical Engine, which is published in a scientific journal.
  • 1843 (October) -Ada's note is published, and she is recognized for her deep understanding of the Analytical Engine and its potential.
  • 1846 - Ada's family moves to a country estate in Lincolnshire.
  • 1848 - Ada works on Babbage's Analytical Engine design, continuing to explore its capabilities.
  • 1852 (November 27) - Ada Lovelace dies in London at the age of 36 due to uterine cancer.
  • Posthumous Recognition - Ada's work and notes are rediscovered later in the 20th century, solidifying her place as a pioneer in computer science.
